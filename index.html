Vegan Chilli

  • Time: 60 minutes
  • Serves: 4 servings
Ingredients Quantity
Yellow onion 1
Cloves garlic 2
Jalapeño 1
Olive oil 1 tbsp
Bulgur 190g
Chipotle flakes 1 tbsp
Oregano 2 tsp
Cumin 2 tsp
Smoked paprika 1/2 tsp
Pinch of cloves 3
Salt to taste
Crushed tomatoes 1 tin
Vegetable broth 500 mL
Kidney or Black beans 1 tin

Step 1: Chop the onion, garlic, and jalapeño into large chunks.

Step 2: Heat a stockpot over medium heat. Add olive oil and onion mixture. Cook until onions are fragrant and transparent, 5 to 6 minutes.

Step 3: Stir in the bulgur and spices, cooking for 1 to 2 minutes or until you can smell the spices. Add in the tomatoes, and 500mL of the vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, reduce to a simmer, cover, and let cook for 8 minutes.

Step 4: After 10 minutes, stir in the beans, cover, and cook for another 10 minutes. Bulgur should be tender. Taste and adjust the salt/seasoning. Add more vegetable broth if a thinner consistency is desired.

Step 5: Serve with sour cream, slices of jalapeños and nachos.

Adapted from: naturally ella and Union of Genius @ Edinburgh

Kimchi Stew

  • Time: 40 minutes
  • Serves: 4 servings
Ingredients Quantity
Kimchi 1 pound
Kimchi brine 1/4 cup
Pork belly 1/2 pound
Tofu 1 package
Green onions 3
Onion 1
Salt 1 tsp
Sugar 2 tsp
Gochugaru 2 tsp
Gochujang 1 tbsp
Sesame oil 1 tsp
Chicken broth 500 mL

Step 1: Place the kimchi and kimchi brine. in a shallow pot. Add pork and onion.

Step 2: Slice 2 green onions diagonally and add them to the pot.

Step 3: Add salt, sugar, hot pepper flakes, and hot pepper paste. Drizzle sesame oil over top and add the chicken stock.

Step 4: Cover and cook for 10 minutes over medium high heat.

Step 5: Open and mix in the seasonings with a spoon. Lay the tofu over top.

Step 6: Cover and cook another 10 to 15 minutes over medium heat.

Step 7: Chop 1 green onion and put it on the top of the stew. Remove from the heat and serve right away with rice.

Adapted from: Maang chi

Banana Bread

  • Time: 70 minutes
Ingredients Quantity
Ripe bananas 2 to 3
Melted butter ~76 g
Baking soda 1 tsp
Salt 1 pinch
Sugar ~100 g
Nuts As desired
Egg 1
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
All purpose flour ~ 192g
Gochujang 1 tbsp
Chocolate chips As desired

Step 1: In a mixing bowl, mash the ripe bananas with a fork until completely smooth. Stir the melted butter into the mashed bananas.

Step 2: Mix in the baking soda and salt.

Step 3: Stir in the sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla extract. Mix in the flour.

Step 4: Pour the butter into your prepared loaf pan. Bake for 50 minutes to 1 hour at 175ºC, or until a tester inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step 5: Open and mix in the seasonings with a spoon. Lay the tofu over top.

Step 6: Remove from oven and let cool in the pan for a few minutes.

Adapted from: different old sources online.
